Basic of Graph Theory

Trial

\(\textbf{Definition. }\)Let \(G\) be a graph. A sequence of distinct edges \(e_1e_2\dots e_k\) is called a trail if we can take a continuous walk in our graph, first walking through the edge \(e_1\), then the edge \(e_2\), and so on. If we start the drawing at the same vertex where we end it, then we say that \(e_1e_2\ldots e_k\) is a closed trail.

Cycle

\(\textbf{Definition: }\)A \(\textit{cycle}\) in a graph is a closed trail that does not touch any vertex twice, except the initial vertex, that must also be the ending vertex.
