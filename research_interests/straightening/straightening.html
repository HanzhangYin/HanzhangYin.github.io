Let \( \lambda = (\lambda_1, \dots, \lambda_k) \) be a partition, and \( \zeta = (\zeta_1, \dots, \zeta_{\lambda_1}) \) its conjugate partition.

Let \( C(\lambda) \) be the group of sequences of permutations \( \underline{\pi} = (\pi_1, \dots , \pi_{\lambda_1} ) \), where each \( \pi_i \in S_{\zeta_i} \).

We define the rearrangement subset of \( C(\lambda) \) associated to \( F, S \in F(\lambda, z) \) to be the set:

\[ C_{F,S}(\lambda) = \{ \underline{\pi} \in C(\lambda) \mid F_{\underline{\pi}} \text{ and } S \text{ have the same row content} \}. \]

Definition 3.1.1. Let \( F, S \in F(\lambda, z) \). We define the rearrangement coefficient associated to these fillings to be:

\[ \mathcal{R}_{F,S} := \sum_{\pi \in C_{F,S}(\lambda)} \text{sgn}(\underline{\pi}) \]

Lemma 3.1.3. Let \( F, T, S \in F(\lambda, z) \). Let \( \sigma, \gamma, \sigma', \gamma' \) be fixed elements of \( C(\lambda) \). Suppose that for all \( \pi \in C_{T,S}(\lambda) \), we have \( \sigma \circ \pi \circ \gamma \in C_{F,S}(\lambda) \). Further, suppose for all \( \pi' \in C_{F,S}(\lambda) \), we have \( \sigma' \circ \pi' \circ \gamma' \in C_{T,S}(\lambda) \). Then:

\[ \mathcal{R}_{F,S} = \text{sgn}(\sigma) \cdot \text{sgn}(\gamma) \cdot \mathcal{R}_{T,S} = \text{sgn}(\sigma') \cdot \text{sgn}(\gamma') \cdot \mathcal{R}_{T,S}. \]

Lemma 3.1.5. Let \( T \in T(\lambda, z) \) and \( \pi \in C(\lambda) \) such that \( \pi \neq (\text{id}, \ldots, \text{id}) \). If \( S = \text{rowsort}(T_\pi) \), then \( S \succ \text{sort}(T) \).

Proof. Suppose that \(i < j\), then we can know that \(s_j \preceq s_i\) according to the definition. Then, we can list the entries of \(s_i\) and \(s_j\) as follows: \[ \begin{aligned} s_i &= [a_1, \dots, a_n] \\ s_j &= [b_1, \dots, b_n] \end{aligned} \] Then there exists \(k\leq n\) such that \(a_k > b_k\) and \(a_i = b_i\) for all \(i < k\) because of the definition. Since \(a_k\) and \(b_k\) should be located at the same square, we can know that they are on the same column. Suppose that the column is \(c\). We list all the entries of \(s_i^c\) and \(s_j^c\) as follows: \[ \begin{aligned} s_i^c &= [a_{i_1}, \dots, a_{i_m}] \\ s_j^c &= [b_{i_1}, \dots, b_{i_m}] \end{aligned} \] Suppose that \(a_{i_l} = a_k\) and \(b_{i_l} = b_k\). Lemma. Suppose that there exists a basis \(\{s_1, \dots, s_n\}\). We have that \(\langle D_{s_i}, D_{s_j}\rangle = 0\) if \(i< j\).

Proof Given that \[ \begin{align} D_{s_1} & = s_1, \\ D_{s_2} & = s_2 - R_{s_2, s_1}D_{s_1} \\ & \vdots \\ D_{s_n} & = s_n - \sum_{k=1}^{n-1} R_{s_n, s_k}D_{s_k}. \end{align} \] By substitution, we have \(D_i = a_1s_1 + a_2s_2 + \dots + s_i\). Then we can know that \[ \begin{align} \langle D_{s_i}, D_{s_j}\rangle &= \langle a_1s_1 + a_2s_2 + \dots + s_i, D_{s_j}\rangle \\ &= \sum_{k=1}^{i-1} a_{k}\langle s_k, D_j\rangle + \langle s_i, D_{s_j}\rangle \\ &= \sum_{k=1}^{i-1} a_{k}R_{s_k, s_j} + R_{s_i,s_j} \\ &= 0. \end{align} \]

Lemma. Suppose that there exists a basis \(\{s_1, \dots, s_n\}\). We have that \(\langle D_{s_i}, D_{s_i}\rangle = 1\).

Proof. For random \(i\), we can know that \(D_{s_i} = s_i - \sum_{k=1}^{i-1} R_{s_i, s_k}D_{s_k}\). Then we can know that \[ \begin{align} \langle D_{s_i}, D_{s_i}\rangle &= \langle s_i - \sum_{k=1}^{i-1} R_{s_i, s_k}D_{s_k}, D_{s_i}\rangle \\ &= \langle s_i, D_{s_i}\rangle - \sum_{k=1}^{i-1} R_{s_i, s_k}\langle D_{s_k}, D_{s_i}\rangle \\ &= 1 - \sum_{k=1}^{i-1} R_{s_i, s_k}R_{s_k, s_i} \\ &= 1. \text{by the previous lemma} \end{align} \]

Lemma. Suppose that there exists a basis \(\{s_1, \dots, s_n\}\). We have that \(\langle D_{s_i}, D_{s_j}\rangle = 0\) when \(i > j\).

Proof. We will prove it by strong induction. Fix \(j\) and we want to show the base case, \(\langle D_{s_{j+1}}, D_{s_j}\rangle = 0\). Since \(D_{s_{j+1}} = s_{j+1} - \sum_{k=1}^{j} R_{s_{j+1}, s_k}D_{s_k}\), we can know that \[ \begin{align} \langle D_{s_{j+1}}, D_{s_j}\rangle &= \langle s_{j+1} - \sum_{k=1}^{j} R_{s_{j+1}, s_k}D_{s_k}, D_{s_j}\rangle \\ &= \langle s_{j+1}, D_{s_j}\rangle - \sum_{k=1}^{j} R_{s_{j+1}, s_k}\langle D_{s_k}, D_{s_j}\rangle \\ &= \langle s_{j+1}, D_{s_j}\rangle - \sum_{k=1}^{j-1} R_{s_{j+1}, s_k}\langle D_{s_k}, D_{s_j}\rangle - R_{s_{j+1}, s_j}\langle D_{s_j}, D_{s_j}\rangle. \end{align} \] According to the previous lemma, we can know that \(\langle D_{s_k}, D_{s_j}\rangle = 0\) when \(k < j\). According to the previous lemma, we can know that \(\langle D_{s_j}, D_{s_j}\rangle = 1\). Then we can know that \[ \begin{align} \langle D_{s_{j+1}}, D_{s_j}\rangle &= \langle s_{j+1}, D_{s_j}\rangle - R_{s_{j+1}, s_j} \\ &= 0. \end{align} \] Then we can know that the base case is true. Now, suppose that it is true for all \(i > j\) and we want to show that it is true for \(i + 1\). Since \(D_{s_{i+1}} = s_{i+1} - \sum_{k=1}^{i} R_{s_{i+1}, s_k}D_{s_k}\), we can know that \[ \begin{align} \langle D_{s_{i+1}}, D_{s_j}\rangle &= \langle s_{i+1} - \sum_{k=1}^{i} R_{s_{i+1}, s_k}D_{s_k}, D_{s_j}\rangle \\ &= \langle s_{i+1}, D_{s_j}\rangle - \sum_{k=1}^{i} R_{s_{i+1}, s_k}\langle D_{s_k}, D_{s_j}\rangle \\ &= \langle s_{i+1}, D_{s_j}\rangle - \sum_{k=1}^{j-1} R_{s_{i+1}, s_k}\langle D_{s_k}, D_{s_j}\rangle - R_{s_{i+1}, s_j}\langle D_{s_j}, D_{s_j}\rangle - \sum_{k=j+1}^{i} R_{s_{i+1}, s_k}\langle D_{s_k}, D_{s_j}\rangle \\ &= \langle s_{i+1}, D_{s_j}\rangle - 0 - R_{s_{i+1}, s_j}\langle D_{s_j}, D_{s_j}\rangle - 0 \text{ by the induction hypothesis} \\ &= 0. \end{align} \] Thus, we can know that the lemma is true.

Corollary. Suppose that there exists a basis \(\{s_1, \dots, s_n\}\). We have that \(\langle D_{s_i}, D_{s_j}\rangle = 0\) when \(i \neq j\).

Proposition. Suppose that there exists a basis \(\{s_1, \dots, s_n\}\). We have that \(\langle s_i, D_{s_j}\rangle = \langle D_{s_j}, s_i\rangle\).

Proof. Firstly, we know that \(D_{s_j} = s_j - \sum_{k=1}^{j-1} R_{s_j, s_k}D_{s_k}\) and \(s_i = R_{s_i, s_1}D_{s_1} + \dots + R_{s_i, s_{i-1}}D_{s_{i-1}} + D_{s_i}\). Then we can know that \[ \begin{align} \langle D_{s_j}, s_i\rangle &= \langle D_{s_j}, R_{s_i, s_1}D_{s_1} + \dots + R_{s_i, s_{i-1}}D_{s_{i-1}} + D_{s_i}\rangle \\ &= \sum_{k=1}^{j-1} R_{s_i, s_k}\langle D_{s_j}, D_{s_k}\rangle + R_{s_i, s_k}\langle D_{s_j}, D_{s_j}\rangle + \sum_{k=j+1}^{i-1} R_{s_i, s_k}\langle D_{s_j}, D_{s_k}\rangle \\ &= R_{s_i, s_k}\langle D_{s_j}, D_{s_j}\rangle \\ &= R_{s_i, s_j} \\ &= \langle s_i, D_{s_j}\rangle. \end{align} \]
