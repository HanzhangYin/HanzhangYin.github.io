Group of Units

Definition

\(\textbf{Definition. }\)Let \(R\) be a ring. Assume \(R\) has an identity \(1 \neq 0\). An element \(u\) of \(R\) is called a unit in \(R\) if there is some \(v\) in \(R\) such that \(uv = vu = 1\). The set of units in \(R\) is denoted \(R^\times\).


Group of Units

\(\textbf{Proposition. }\)Let \(R\) be a ring. Assume \(R\) has an identity \(1 \neq 0\). Then \(R^\times\) is a group under multiplication.
