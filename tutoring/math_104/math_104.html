Factoring

Difference in Squares

\[ A^2 - B^2 = (A+B)\cdot(A-B) \]

Sum of Squares

\[ A^2 + B^2 \text{ cannot be factored } \]

Sum of Cubes

\[ A^3 + B^3 = (A+B)\cdot(A^2 - AB + B^2) \]

Difference of Cubes

\[ A^3 - B^3 = (A-B)\cdot(A^2 + AB + B^2) \]

Binomial Expansion

\[ (A+B)^2 = (A+B)\cdot(A+B) = A^2 + 2AB + B^2 \] \[ (A-B)^2 = (A-B)\cdot(A-B) = A^2 - 2AB + B^2 \]

Quadratic Formula

\[ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \]

Completing the Square

We have \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\). Besides the quadratic formula, we can also complete the square to solve the equation. Here we treat \(a\neq 0\) since it will become a linear equation once \(a = 0\) and we know how to solve a linear equation. - Divide by \(a\): \[x^2 + \frac{b}{a}x + \frac{c}{a} = 0\] - Move the constant term to the right side: \[x^2 + \frac{b}{a}x = -\frac{c}{a}\] - Add \((\frac{b}{2a})^2\) to both sides: \[x^2 + \frac{b}{a}x + \left(\frac{b}{2a}\right)^2 = -\frac{c}{a} + (\frac{b}{2a})^2\] - Factor the left side: \[\left(x + \frac{b}{2a}\right)^2 = -\frac{c}{a} + \left(\frac{b}{2a}\right)^2\] - Solve for \(x\): \[x = -\frac{b}{2a} \pm \sqrt{-\frac{c}{a} + (\frac{b}{2a})^2}\] - Be more specific, the two solutions are \[x_1 = -\frac{b}{2a} + \sqrt{-\frac{c}{a} + (\frac{b}{2a})^2}\] \[x_2 = -\frac{b}{2a} - \sqrt{-\frac{c}{a} + (\frac{b}{2a})^2}\]
