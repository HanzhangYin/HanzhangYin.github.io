Title

Norm

\(\textbf{Definition. }\) Any function \(N: R\to \mathbb{Z}^+ \cup \{0\}\) with \(N(0) = 0\) is called a \(\textit{norm}\) on the integral domain \(R\). If \(N(a) > 0\) for \(a \neq 0\) define \(N\) to be a positive norm.


\(\textbf{Definition. }\)The integral domain \(R\) is said to be a Euclidean Domain (or possess a Division Algorithm) if there is a norm \(N\) on \(R\) such that for any two elements \(a\) and \(b\) of \(R\) with \(b \neq 0\) there exist elements \(q\) and \(r\) in \(R\) with $$ a=qb+r \qquad \text{with }r = 0\text{ or }N(r) \lt N(b). $$ The element \(q \)is called the \(\textit{quotient}\) and the element \(r\) the \(\textit{remainder}\) of the division.
