Norm

\(\textbf{Definition. }\) Any function \(N: R\to \mathbb{Z}^+ \cup \{0\}\) with \(N(0) = 0\) is called a \(\textit{norm}\) on the integral domain \(R\). If \(N(a) > 0\) for \(a \neq 0\) define \(N\) to be a positive norm.


Euclidean Domain

\(\textbf{Definition. }\)The integral domain \(R\) is said to be a Euclidean Domain (or possess a Division Algorithm) if there is a norm \(N\) on \(R\) such that for any two elements \(a\) and \(b\) of \(R\) with \(b \neq 0\) there exist elements \(q\) and \(r\) in \(R\) with $$ a=qb+r \qquad \text{with }r = 0\text{ or }N(r) \lt N(b). $$ The element \(q \) is called the \(\textit{quotient}\) and the element \(r\) the \(\textit{remainder}\) of the division.


Let \(R\) be an integral domain.

Irreducibility

Suppose \(r\in R\) is nonzero and is not a unit. Then \(r\) is called irreducible in \(R\) if whenever \(r = ab\) with \(a, b\in R\), at least one of \(a\) or \(b\) must be a unit in \(R\). Otherwise \(r\) is said to be reducible.

Prime

The nonzero element \(p\in R\) is called prime in \(R\) if the ideal \((p)\) generated by \(p\) is a prime ideal. In other words, a nonzero element \(p\) is a prime if it is not a unit and whenever \(p\mid ab\) for any \(a, b\in R\), then either \(p\mid a\) or \(p\mid b\).

Associate

Two elements \(a\) and \(b\) of \(R\) differing by a unit are said to be associate in \(R\) (i.e., \(a = ub\) for some unit \(u\) in \(R\)).


Big Picture

\(\text{fields}\subset \text{Euclidean Domains}\subset \text{P.I.D.s}\subset \text{U.F.D.s}\subset \text{integral domains}\)


\(\textbf{Theorem. }\)Every Principal Ideal Domain is a Unique Factorization Domain.

\(\textbf{Proof. }\)Let \(R\) to be a Principal Ideal Domain and let \(r\) be a nonzero element of R which is not a unit. We must show first that r can be written as a finite product of irreducible elements of \(R\) and then we must verify that this decomposition is unique up to units.
