Chain Rule

The chain rule is a formula for computing the derivative of the composition of two or more functions. It is a key rule for finding the derivative of composite functions. The chain rule states that the derivative of \[ f(g(x)) is f'(g(x)) * g'(x). \] In other words, the derivative of the outer function evaluated at the inner function times the derivative of the inner function.
