What is the definition of a \(\text{ Simple Group}\)?
A simple group is a nontrivial group whose only normal subgroups are the trivial group and the group itself (i.e. \(\{e\}\) and \(G\)).
How to tell a subgroup is normal? (Not only one method)
  • \(N\leq G\) is normal if and only if \(gng^{-1}\in N) for all \(g\in G\) and \(n\in N\).
  • A subgroup \(N\) of the group \(G\) is normal if and only if it is the kernel of some homomorphism.
