Field

\(\textbf{Definition: }\)Any set that satisfies all following axioms is called a field.

  • \(\textbf{Commutativity of Addition. }\)For all real numbers \(a\) and \(b\), \[ a+b=b+a. \]
  • \(\textbf{Associativity of Addition. }\)For all real numbers \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\), \[ (a+b)+c=a+(b+c). \]
  • \(\textbf{Additive Identity. }\)There exists a real number \(0\) such that for all real numbers \(a\), \[ a+0=0+a=a. \]
  • \(\textbf{Additive Inverse. }\)For every real number $a$, there exists a real number \(-a\) such that \[ a+(-a)=(-a)+a=0. \]
  • \(\textbf{Commutativity of Multiplication. }\)For all real numbers \(a\) and \(b\), \[ a\cdot b=b\cdot a. \]
  • \(\textbf{Associativity of Multiplication. }\)For all real numbers \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\), \[ (a\cdot b)\cdot c=a\cdot (b\cdot c). \]
  • \(\textbf{Multiplicative Identity. }\)There exists a real number \(1\) such that for all real numbers \(a\), \[ a\cdot 1=1\cdot a=a. \]
  • \(\textbf{Multiplicative Inverse. }\)For every real number $a\neq 0$, there exists a real number \(a^{-1}\) such that \[ a\cdot a^{-1}=a^{-1}\cdot a=1. \]
  • \(\textbf{Distributivity. }\)For all real numbers \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\), \[ a\cdot (b+c)=a\cdot b+a\cdot c. \]
  • \(\textbf{Nontriviality Assumption. }\) \[ 1\neq 0. \]


Problem 1

\(\textbf{Problem: }\)For \(a\neq 0\) and $b\neq 0$, show that $(ab)^{-1}=a^{-1}b^{-1}$.

\(\textbf{Proof. }\)Assume that \(a, b\neq 0\) and are in $\mathbb{R}$, \[ (ab)(ab)^{-1} = 1, \] \[ a a^{-1}bb^{-1} = 1. \] For commutativity of multiplication, \[ a b a^{-1}b^{-1} = 1. \] Thus, \[ ab(ab)^{-1} = a b a^{-1}b^{-1}. \] Therefore, \[ (ab)^{-1} = a^{-1}b^{-1}. \] \[ \tag*{$\square$} \]
