Quadratic Form

Positive Definite

\(\textbf{Definition. }\)A quadratic form \(q\) is positive definite if \(q(v) > 0\).

\(\textbf{Definition. }\)A quadratic form \(q\) is positive semi-definite if \(q(v) \geq 0\).

\(\textbf{Definition. }\)A quadratic form \(q\) is negative definite if \(-q(v) > 0\).

\(\textbf{Definition. }\)A quadratic form \(q\) is negative semi-definite if \(-q(v) \geq 0\).


\(\textbf{Theorme 3. }\)A quadratic form \(f\) is negative definite or positive definite if and only if discriminant of \(\f\) is less than zero.


\(\textbf{Definition. }\)A strict vector \(v\) is a primitive vector distinct from (\(-v\)).

\(\textbf{Definition. }\)A lax vector \(v\) is a primitive vector identify it with (\(-v\)).

\(\textbf{Definition. }\)A strict basis is a pair \((u, v)\in\mathbb{Z}^2\times\mathbb{Z}^2\) that are primitive and generate \(\mathbb{Z}^2\).

\(\textbf{Definition. }\)A lax basis is a basis \((\pm u, \pm v)\in\mathbb{Z}^2\times\mathbb{Z}^2\) where \((u, v)\) are strict basis.

\(\textbf{Definition. }\)A strict super basis is a triple \((u, v, w)\) such that \(u+v+w=0\) and \((u, v)\) is a strict basis.

\(\textbf{Definition. }\)A lax super basis is a triple \(\pm u, \pm v, \pm w\) where \((u, v, w)\) is a strict super basis.

\(\textbf{Theorem 4. }\)Every Lax superbasis \(\{\pm u, \pm v, \pm w\}\) is contained in exatly 2 super bases.
