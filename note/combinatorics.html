Pigeon-hole Principle References

Combinatorics

Definition

\(\textbf{Theorem 1.1. (Pigeon-hole Principle).}\) Let \(A\) and \(B\) be two finite sets and \(f\) be a function from \(A\) to \(B\). If \(|A| > |B|\), then \(f\) is not one-to-one. (i.e. there exists \(a_1, a_2 \in A\) such that \(a_1 \neq a_2\) and \(f(a_1) = f(a_2)\)).

\(\textbf{Proof. }\)We will prove it with mathematical contrapositive. We need to show that if \(f\) is one-to-one, then \(|A| \leq |B|\). Suppose that \(f\) is one-to-one. Let \(A = \{a_1, a_2, \dots, a_n\}\), which implies that \(|A| = n\). Then \(f(A) = \{f(a_1), f(a_2), \dots, f(a_n)\}\) and \(|f(A)| = n\). Therefore, \(A\subset B\). \[ \tag*{$\square$} \]

\(\textbf{Theorem 1.1. (Pigeon-hole Principle, general version).}\) Let \(A\) and \(B\) be two finite sets with \(|A| = n\) and \(|B| = m\). Let \(r\) be a positive integer and let \(f\) be a function from \(A\) to \(B\). If \(n > rm\), then there exists \(b \in B\) such that \[ \(|f^{-1}(b)| \geq r+1\), \] where \(f^{-1}(b) = \{a \in A : f(a) = b\}\).
